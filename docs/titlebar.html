Title Bar and Buttons

Here's all CSS for setting navication bars and buttons. You can change the title font independently by changing the variable altfont. The classes for the title bar start at line 485 in the CSS file, and the button classes are at line 51.

.topbar, .toptitle, and .topbutton

.toptitle and .topbutton must go inside .topbar for the bar to work and the CSS to not spread to the rest of the page. .toptitle puts your page title in the top left corner, taking up 50% of screen width. .topbutton puts your links or buttons opposite to your title. Your HTML should look something like this:

<div class="topbar">
    <div class="toptitle">
        <h1><a class="nodeco" href="https://example.com"><img src="image.png" alt="Logo" width="30" height="30">Name</a></h1>
    </div>
    <div class="topbutton">
        <h2><a class="nodeco" href="link">Your Link</a></h2>
    </div>
</div>

.dropdown, .dropbtn, .dropdown-content

Everything must go inside .dropdown for the dropdown to work. .dropbtn is the button you hover over. .dropdown-content is what the links go in. The HTML should look something like this:

<div class="dropdown">
    <button class="dropbtn">Dropdown</button>
    <div class="dropdown-content">
        <a href="Link">Name</a>
        <a href="Link">Name</a>
        <a href="Link">Name</a>
    </div>
</div>

.fancybutton1

Styles your button so when you hover over it, it highlights and an arrow appears. Your HTML should look something like this:

<a href="https://example.com"><button class="learn-more">
  <spanclass="circle"aria-hidden="true">
    <spanclass="icon arrow"></span>
  </span>
  <spanclass="button-text">Your Link</span>
</button></a>

.fancybutton2

Styles your button so when you hover over it, it fills in from below. Your HTML just needs the class, like this:

<a href="https://example.com"><button class="fancybutton2">Your Link</button></a>
