Tags

Here's what we default to the HTML tags (like <a>), and where you need to do something special to make them work. If you're looking in the CSS file, they start at line 18.

<html>

Sets font, margins, background, and text color.

<a>

Underlines and makes links the same color as normal text.

<img>

Makes images undraggable and unselectable

<footer>

Adds wider margins, then centers and shrinks text.

<button>

Colors buttons and gives them padding and margins.

<input> and variations

Gives inputs backgrounds and borders and gives them padding and margins.

<input type="radio">

Styles your Radio inputs in a really fancy way.

<div class="radio">
    <input type="radio" id="ThingOne" name="ListName" value="ThingOne">
    <label for="ThingOne"> Thing One Name</label>
    <input type="radio" id="ThingTwo" name="ListName" value="ThingTwo">
    <label for="ThingOne"> Thing Two Name</label>
    <input type="radio" id="ThingThree" name="ListName" value="ThingThree">
    <label for="ThingThree"> Thing Three Name</label>
</div>

<table>, <th>, <td>

Styles your tables with some very snazzy borders and backgrounds.
